# INTRODUCTION

This guide is designed to help you organize engaging, fun-filled neighborhood bike events that bring communities together while promoting cycling culture. Whether you're planning a "Neighborhood Bike Bash," "Community Bike Celebration," or "Spring Bike Festival," this resource will walk you through the essential elements of creating a successful local event centered around bicycling.

> Remember: Great community bike events combine practical value (like free repairs) with fun activities that get people excited about cycling in their neighborhood.
{.is-success}

# EVENT OVERVIEW

**Format:** 3-4 hour outdoor community gathering

**Setting:** Neighborhood park, community center, school playground, or closed-off street section

**Participants:** 
- 30-200+ community members of all ages
- 5-15 volunteers/organizers

> Events like this grow over time. We've hosted events with more volunteers than participants. Don't get discouraged! It takes practice and multiple repeates to grow an event.
{.is-info}

**Purpose:** To celebrate bicycle culture, provide practical bike services, teach bike safety, build community connections, and encourage more neighborhood cycling through an accessible, family-friendly event.

This event is designed to be:
- **Inclusive:** Welcoming cyclists of all ages and skill levels
- **Practical:** Offering tangible benefits like basic repairs
- **Educational:** Teaching essential cycling skills and safety
- **Festive:** Creating a celebratory atmosphere around cycling
- **Community-Building:** Strengthening neighborhood connections

> Spring is an ideal time for these events, as people are getting bikes ready for the season!
{.is-info}

# DESIRED OUTCOMES

By the end of your bike celebration, you should achieve the following:

1. **Community Engagement:** Residents feel more connected to their neighborhood and each other
2. **Increased Cycling Interest:** Participants feel inspired to ride more frequently
3. **Practical Support:** Bikes are repaired, tuned up, and ready for the season
4. **Safety Education:** Participants (especially children) learn essential cycling safety skills
5. **Future Planning:** Connections made for potential cycling advocacy or future events

# PRE-EVENT PLANNING

## 2 Months Before
- [ ] Form organizing committee (3-5 people is ideal)
- [ ] Set date, time, and secure location. Many cities will require a special use permit, even if hosted in the parking lot on private property.
- [ ] Develop budget and identify funding sources (if needed)
- [ ] Connect with local bike shops for potential partnerships

1. The **family-friendly** nature of the event

5. The **seasonal timing** (getting ready for spring/summer riding)

# ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

For a successful bike event, assign the following roles to your team:

## Event Coordinator
**Responsibilities:**
- Oversees entire event planning and execution
- Coordinates with all team leads
- Manages budget and resources
- Ensures proper permits and permissions
- Makes day-of decisions as needed

**Skills needed:** Strong organizational and leadership abilities, experience with event planning, good decision-making under pressure

## Mechanical Support Lead
**Responsibilities:**
- Recruits volunteer mechanics
- Organizes repair station layout and workflow
- Ensures proper tools and basic parts are available
- Manages repair sign-ups during the event
- Provides guidance on repair limitations

**Skills needed:** Bicycle mechanical knowledge, connections to local bike shop community, organizational skills

## Activity Coordinator
**Responsibilities:**
- Plans and oversees specific activity (e.g. Bike Rodeo, Bike Parade, Bike Games)
- Recruits and trains activity station volunteers
- Ensures all supplies are prepared and available
- Adapts activities based on turnout and flow
- Keeps activities running on schedule

**Skills needed:** Experience with group activities, creativity, enthusiasm, ability to engage with children and families

## Volunteer Coordinator
**Responsibilities:**
- Recruits all event volunteers
- Creates volunteer schedule and assignments
- Conducts volunteer orientation/training
- Serves as point person for volunteer questions
- Ensures volunteers have necessary supplies and breaks

**Skills needed:** Strong people skills, organizational ability, clear communication

## Marketing/Outreach Lead
**Responsibilities:**
- Creates and distributes all promotional materials
- Manages social media and online presence
- Conducts community outreach
- Coordinates with local media
- Documents event with photos/video

**Skills needed:** Marketing experience, social media savvy, good writing skills, community connections

> For smaller events, individuals can take on multiple roles, but be sure all key responsibilities are assigned.

# EVENT ACTIVITIES

## 1. Bike Repair Station

**Purpose:** Provide practical bike maintenance, get bikes ready for the season, and teach basic mechanical skills

**Setup Needs:**
- Repair stands, tools, parts
- Mechanics with various skill levels
- Shade/shelter
- Check-in system for repairs

**Instructions:**
1. When possible, invite attendees to learn the repairs with your bike mechanics.
2. Establish a triage system to handle simpler repairs first
3. Set clear expectations about repair limitations
4. Include a self-service station with pumps and basic tools
5. Offer simple maintenance demonstrations throughout the event

**Volunteer Tips:**
- Have mechanics bring their own toolkits if possible
- Pair experienced mechanics with beginners for mentoring
- Have a "bike wash" station to clean dirty bikes before repairs

## 2. Bike Safety Rodeo

**Purpose:** Teach essential cycling safety skills in a fun, interactive format for children

**Setup Needs:**
- Open paved area
- Course materials (cones, chalk, signs)
- Volunteer instructors
- Safety handouts

**Course Stations:**
View our [bike rodeo page](/education/rodeo) or purchase a [Youth Skills Instructor Manual](https://shop.bikeleague.org/collections/bike-education-materials-1) from the League of American Bicyclists for more details. A basic course usually includes:
1. **Helmet Fitting** - Proper fitting and importance
2. **ABC Quick Check** - Air, Brakes, Chain/Crank, Quick Release safety check
3. **Starting/Stopping Practice** - Control basics
4. **Straight Line Riding** - Balance and control
5. **Signaling Practice** - Hand signals while maintaining control
6. **Obstacle Navigation** - Avoiding hazards
7. **Intersection Awareness** - Looking and crossing safely

**Instructions:**
1. Design course based on available space and expected age ranges
2. Create "completion cards" that get stamped at each station
3. Offer "certificates" for children who complete all stations
4. Consider different tracks for different age/skill levels

**Volunteer Tips:**
- Use teen volunteers to demonstrate skills to younger children
- Keep instructions simple and demonstrations clear
- Celebrate small improvements
- Have spare helmets available for fitting demonstrations

## 3. Decorated Bike Parade

**Purpose:** Create festive community activity and visible celebration of cycling

**Setup Needs:**
- Decoration station with various materials
- Planned route (0.5-1 mile, traffic-controlled)
- Parade marshals/leaders
- Gathering area for assembly

**Instructions:**
1. Set up decoration station 2+ hours before parade
2. Create clear map of route for participants
3. Designate parade leaders, middle marshals, and sweep riders
4. Consider a theme for decorations (seasons, community pride, etc.)
5. Arrange for any traffic control needs with local authorities
6. Plan for inclusive participation (walking option for those without bikes)

**Volunteer Tips:**
- Take photos at a designated spot along the route
- Have volunteer "safety riders" throughout the parade
- Use walkie-talkies for communication among parade staff
- Have a contingency plan for route if needed
- Consider music/noisemakers to make the parade more festive

## 4. Cycling Games and Activities

**Purpose:** Create fun, engaging activities that build bike handling skills

**Game Options:**
1. **Slow Race** - Last person to reach finish line without putting foot down wins
2. **Bike Limbo** - How low can you go while still riding? *P(roblematic with most insurance)*
3. **Obstacle Course** - Navigate through fun challenges
4. **Bike Bingo** - Find items throughout the event area while riding
5. **Target Toss** - Throw soft objects into targets while riding past
6. **Balance Contest** - Track stand competition (who can balance in place longest)
7. **Bike Bowling** - Knock down pins with a ball while riding
8. **Water Carry** - Transport water in a cup through a course without spilling

**Setup Needs:**
- Designated game areas
- Game-specific materials
- Volunteer facilitators
- Small prizes

**Instructions:**
1. Create clear, simple rules for each game
2. Establish appropriate age groups if needed
3. Keep games short so multiple people can participate
4. Ensure activities build skills while being fun

## 5. Educational Workshops

**Purpose:** Share knowledge and build community skills

**Workshop Ideas:**
1. **Basic Bike Maintenance** (15-20 minutes)
   - Fixing a flat
   - Adjusting brakes
   - Cleaning and lubing a chain
   
2. **Urban Cycling Skills** (15-20 minutes)
   - Safe lane positioning
   - Navigating intersections
   - Communication with drivers
   
3. **Family Cycling Tips** (15-20 minutes)
   - Equipment options for riding with kids
   - Route planning for families
   - Building children's confidence
   
4. **Bike Commuting 101** (15-20 minutes)
   - Essential gear
   - Route planning
   - Weather preparation

**Setup Needs:**
- Quiet space with some seating
- Demo bike and tools
- Handouts or take-home resources
- Knowledgeable presenters

**Volunteer Tips:**
- Keep workshops brief and focused
- Use visual demonstrations
- Leave time for Q&A
- Provide resource list for further learning

## 6. Additional Activity Ideas

1. **Bike Swap Area** - Designated space for trading/selling bikes and parts
2. **Bike Portrait Booth** - Photo station with your bike and logos of sponsors or your organization as backdrop. Costume pieces like big glasses, wigs, or fun hats / helmets are a great addition.
3. **Community Mapping** - Map for community members to mark and identify good/bad cycling routes.
4. **Bike-Powered Activities** - Attach a dynamo to a stationary bike (especially kid sized bikes) to power small electronics like speakers or a bulb.
5. **Bike Art** - Create art using bike parts or bike-themed crafts
6. **Bike Registry** - Register bikes with local police for theft prevention
7. **E-Bike Demonstrations** - Intro to electric-assist bikes
8. **Adaptive Cycling Showcase** - Featuring bikes for various abilities
9. **Bike History Display** - Evolution of bicycles with examples
10. **Local Bike Route Planning** - Help finding safe routes in your area
11. **Bike-Themed Face Painting** - For younger children
12. **Cycling Advocacy Information** - Local initiatives and how to get involved
13. **Bike Trailer Demonstrations** - Cargo options for utilitarian cycling

# WEATHER CONTINGENCY PLANS

Outdoor events require backup plans:

1. **Light Rain**
   - Have canopy tents for key stations
   - Prepare plastic coverings for materials
   - Focus on covered activities
   
2. **Heavy Rain/Storms**
   - Set a clear cancellation/postponement policy
   - Have an indoor backup location if possible
   - Communicate changes promptly via social media/email
   - Consider a rain date option
   
3. **Excessive Heat**
   - Provide shade structures
   - Ensure water stations
   - Schedule high-activity events for cooler parts of day
   - Have cooling stations
   
4. **Wind**
   - Secure all tents and materials
   - Have weights for paper materials
   - Modify bike parade route if needed to avoid wind tunnels

# EVALUATION METRICS

To measure success and improve future events, track these key metrics:

1. **Attendance**
   - Total participants
   - Demographics (ages, neighborhoods)
   - First-time vs. returning participants
   
2. **Activity Participation**
   - Number of bikes repaired
   - Children completing safety rodeo
   - Parade participants
   - Workshop attendance
   
3. **Community Impact**
   - New connections formed
   - Local organizations involved
   - Media coverage obtained
   - Social media engagement
   
4. **Volunteer Engagement**
   - Number of volunteers
   - Hours contributed
   - Volunteer satisfaction
   - New skills developed
   
5. **Practical Outcomes**
   - Bikes made road-ready
   - Safety issues addressed
   - New cyclists engaged
   - Cycling resources distributed

# CONCLUSION

A successful neighborhood bike event does more than just fill a day with activities—it strengthens community bonds, encourages active transportation, builds skills, and creates lasting memories. By providing both practical value (through repairs and education) and joyful experiences (through parades and games), you're contributing to a more vibrant, connected, and bike-friendly neighborhood.

Remember that even small events can have significant impact. Start where you are, use what you have, and celebrate the universal joy of cycling with your neighbors. Each successful event builds momentum for a more bike-friendly community.

Happy planning, and enjoy the ride!
