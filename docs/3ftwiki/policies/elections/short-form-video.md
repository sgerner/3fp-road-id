In today's media landscape, short-form video (like Instagram Reels, TikToks, and YouTube Shorts) is one of the most effective ways to reach a broad audience. By creating engaging, bite-sized video content with candidates, you can educate voters who may never read a full questionnaire or listen to a long-form podcast. The key is to be creative, concise, and, as always, strictly nonpartisan.

# Why Go Short-Form?

- **Reach:** Short-form video algorithms are designed for viral reach, exposing your content to a much wider audience than your existing followers.
- **Engagement:** These videos are highly engaging and shareable, making it easy for your supporters to spread the word.
- **Accessibility:** A 60-second video is an easy, low-commitment way for a voter to get a quick impression of a candidate.
- **Authenticity:** A less formal, on-the-street video can often feel more authentic and less rehearsed than a polished studio interview.

# Creative, Nonpartisan Video Concepts

You must offer the _exact same opportunity_ to every candidate. The easiest way to do this is to have a single, repeatable format that you can film with each candidate who agrees to participate.

Here are a few proven concepts:

**1. "3 Questions in 60 Seconds"**

- **The Concept:** Ask each candidate the same three rapid-fire, issue-based questions. The challenge is for them to give a concise answer for each within the time limit.
- **Why it Works:** It's fast-paced, fair, and easily comparable. It respects the candidate's time while creating engaging content.
- **Pro-Tip:** Film this on a well-known street corner or in front of a relevant landmark to give it a sense of place.

**2. "Explain This Location"**

- **The Concept:** Take the candidate to a specific location in your city—a dangerous intersection, a successful new bike lane, a transit stop—and ask them to explain what they see and what they would do to improve it.
- **Why it Works:** It grounds the conversation in a real-world example that voters can see and understand. It tests the candidate's ability to think on their feet and apply their knowledge.

**3. "Finish the Sentence"**

- **The Concept:** Give the candidate a series of prompts related to your issues and ask them to complete the sentence.
  - "My vision for transportation in our city is..."
  - "A safe street is one where..."
  - "The first thing I would do to improve bicycling is..."
- **Why it Works:** It's a fun, creative format that encourages concise, memorable answers.

# Production and Publication

- **Keep it Simple:** You don't need fancy equipment. A modern smartphone with a clean lens and a simple $20-$30 bluetooth external microphone will produce excellent results.
- **Film Vertically:** Always film in a vertical 9:16 aspect ratio for these platforms.
- **Use On-Screen Text:** Add captions or key takeaways as on-screen text, as many users watch videos with the sound off.
- **Tag the Candidates:** When you post the videos, be sure to tag the candidates' official campaign accounts.
