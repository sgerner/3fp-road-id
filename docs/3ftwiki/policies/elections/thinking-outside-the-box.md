While forums and questionnaires are essential, don't be afraid to get creative with your candidate events. A unique, fun, and memorable event can generate buzz, attract a different audience, and leave a lasting impression on candidates. As long as you follow the golden rule of nonpartisanship—offering the same opportunity to all candidates—the possibilities are endless.

# Why Get Creative?

- **Stand Out from the Crowd:** During a busy election season, a creative event can cut through the noise of traditional political events.
- **Attract a Wider Audience:** A fun event can attract people who are not typically interested in politics.
- **Show a Different Side of Candidates:** A less formal setting can allow a candidate's personality to shine through.
- **Generate Media Interest:** A unique event concept is more likely to get media coverage than a standard forum.

# Creative Event Ideas

Here are a few "out-of-the-box" ideas to get you started. Remember to adapt them to your community and your organization's personality.

## 1. Candidate Karaoke

- **The Concept:** Host an event at a local bar or community space with a karaoke setup. Invite all the candidates. Each candidate gets to sing one song of their choice and is also given 3-5 minutes on the microphone to talk about their platform.
- **Why it Works:** It's fun, disarming, and shows a candidate's willingness to not take themselves too seriously. It's a great way to see how they connect with people in a casual, high-energy environment.
- **Nonpartisan Rule:** You must give every candidate the same amount of time on the mic to speak, and the same number of songs.

## 2. Slow Roll Scavenger Hunt

- **The Concept:** Organize a slow, family-friendly bike ride that incorporates a scavenger hunt. The clues for the scavenger hunt can be related to your advocacy issues or local history. Invite all candidates to form teams with community members and participate.
- **Why it Works:** It's an interactive and engaging way to get candidates to explore the community by bike. It encourages teamwork and problem-solving.
- **Nonpartisan Rule:** Ensure the scavenger hunt is fair and that all teams have an equal chance of winning. The prize should be something small and fun.

## 3. Family-Friendly "Decorate Your Bike" Party

- **The Concept:** Host a party in a park. Provide craft supplies—streamers, balloons, cardboard, markers—and encourage kids and adults to decorate their bikes. Invite all candidates to come and mingle, and perhaps act as "celebrity judges" for a bike decorating contest.
- **Why it Works:** It's a great way to engage families and to create a positive, festive atmosphere. It associates the candidates with a fun, family-friendly activity.
- **Nonpartisan Rule:** If you have a contest, ensure the judging criteria are clear and fair. Give each candidate a chance to say a few words to the crowd.

# The Key to Success: Planning and Nonpartisanship

No matter how creative your event is, the fundamentals of good planning still apply. You need to have a clear goal, a solid plan, and a commitment to treating all candidates fairly and equally. A well-executed creative event can be one of the most impactful things you do all election season.
